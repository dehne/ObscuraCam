Background

People can use ObscuraCam to take photos when Port Townsend's Mobile Camera Obscura is in use at an event. It's not reasonable to expect privacy at such events.

When a person connects their device to ObscuraCam's WiFi, visits its built in website and then clicks a button, ObscuraCam takes a photo of the screen inside the camera obscura. After it does so it sends the photo to the connected device for the person to see and, if they desire, to save. It also keeps a copy of the photo for The Raw Art Collective and others to use.

The Raw Art Collective doesn't sell the photos ObscuraCam takes to advertisers or data brokers, but it also doesn't take measures to protect the photos from public viewing or reuse. You should assume that photos you or others take with ObscuraCam are likely find their way into the public view.
